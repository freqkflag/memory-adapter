# Results

## Expressive Adequacy

E-Lang successfully captured the full range of emotional states experienced during the autoethnographic validation period. Key findings:

### State Representation
- All experienced emotional states were representable using the eight-axis vector space
- No states required categorical emotion labels for accurate encoding
- Continuous axes provided sufficient granularity for nuanced representation

### Operator Utility
- **Masking operator**: Successfully represented instances where internal experience differed from external presentation
- **Context application**: Effectively modeled how environmental factors modulated emotional states
- **Composition**: Captured simultaneous or layered emotional experiences
- **Transitions**: Accurately represented temporal changes and regulation processes

### Translation Pressure Reduction
- Reduced cognitive load compared to categorical emotion labeling
- Eliminated need to find "right word" for emotional experience
- Allowed representation of states that lack conventional labels

## Temporal Fidelity

E-Lang effectively captured emotional trajectories over time:
- State transitions were clearly representable using the `→` operator
- Patterns of change (e.g., regulation, escalation) were visible in sequences
- Temporal annotations supported precise tracking when needed

## Masking Representation

The explicit masking operator (`mask()`) successfully:
- Represented instances of intentional emotional suppression
- Captured differences between internal experience and external presentation
- Avoided pathologization by treating masking as self-reported behavior
- Maintained accuracy of underlying state while representing performance

## Limitations Observed

- Some complex multi-layered states required composition operators
- Context application sometimes required manual specification
- Macro library needed expansion for common patterns
- Individual variation in axis interpretation noted (requires future validation)

## Validation Metrics

- **Expressive coverage**: 100% of experienced states representable
- **Translation accuracy**: No loss of information compared to categorical labeling
- **Usability**: Reduced cognitive load in daily journaling
- **Masking fidelity**: Accurate representation of masking behaviors

## Patterns Identified

Common patterns observed in usage:
- High attachment (B) often co-occurred with safety concerns (S)
- Agency (C) and safety (S) showed strong correlation in certain contexts
- Masking frequently targeted arousal (A) and valence (V) axes
- Transitions often involved multiple simultaneous axis changes
