# Positionality Statement

## Researcher Positionality

I am a neurodivergent researcher (autism and ADHD) who developed E-Lang through lived use and formalization. This positionality is central to the research:

### Lived Experience
- Direct experience with neurodivergent emotional processing
- Personal understanding of masking, translation pressure, and categorical emotion limitations
- Grounded knowledge of what emotional representation systems fail to capture

### Epistemic Perspective
- First-person experience as valid knowledge source
- Rejection of neurotypical assumptions about emotional processing
- Commitment to neurodiversity-affirming approaches

### Potential Biases
- May over-weight personal experience patterns
- Cultural and individual variation may not be fully represented
- Single perspective limits generalizability

### Mitigation
- Explicit acknowledgment of positionality
- Design for extensibility and individual variation
- Invitation for community validation and extension
- Separation of representation (objective) from interpretation (subjective)

## Research Ethics

This work is conducted with explicit attention to:
- Autonomy: Users author their own emotional representations
- Consent: No inference or assumption without explicit user input
- Agency: Users control interpretation and disclosure
- Resistance to surveillance: Design intentionally resists coercive use

## Transparency

This positionality statement is provided to:
- Clarify the epistemic foundation of the work
- Acknowledge limitations and biases
- Invite critical engagement and extension
- Model transparency in neurodiversity research
